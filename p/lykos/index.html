Our Mission

Playbook

The Lykos 7-Step Framework
Step 1

Validation & Problem Statement

Identify and validate a real problem. Craft a clear problem statement and test your proposed solution with early users to confirm interest.

Step 2

Business Model and Feasibility Analysis

Check if the Business is Feasible. Can the business eventually sustain itself. Identify what is necessary for the business to operate.

Step 3

Legal Entity Formation

Choose and register your legal structure. Set up banking, understand IP basics, and get your cap table in place..

Step 4

Branding & Mission Statement

Define your mission, vision, and positioning. Develop your brand identity—logo, tone, and story that resonates.

Step 5

Proof of Concept & MVP

Build a simple MVP or prototype. Launch to early users, gather feedback, and iterate quickly on core features.

Step 6

Pitch Deck Creation

Craft a concise, compelling pitch deck—highlighting problem, solution, traction, team, and market opportunity to captivate investors.

Step 7

Funding Opportunities Identification

Identify grants, angel investors, and pre-seed funds. Get personalized intros and advice on when and how to pitch.

Have a question? Check out the FAQ

Answers to common questions about Lykos program.

Who can apply to Lykos?

Any student founder with a startup idea—solo or with a small team. Whether you’re in your first year or finishing your degree. You don’t need a team, a product, or any prior experience—just a clear problem you care about and the willingness to work on it.

Is there a cost to join the program?

We believe in accessible entrepreneurship. There is no upfront fee for our core program—Lykos takes a small equity stake only if and when your startup raises funding through our introductions. Otherwise, participation is free.

How long is the Lykos program?

The core program runs for 12 weeks, covering our 7-step playbook. You’ll have structured check-ins, mentorship sessions, and milestones to hit each phase. Afterward, alumni get ongoing access to the Lykos community and resources.

Do I need a technical background to join?

No. Lykos welcomes founders from all disciplines—engineering, design, business, or any other field. If you need technical support, we’ll connect you with mentors or student developers who can help build your MVP.

Can I join with just an idea?

Absolutely. Our first step is validating your problem and solution. Even if you only have a rough concept, we’ll help you shape it into a clear problem statement and roadmap for building a prototype.

How do I get funding through Lykos?

Once you’ve built your MVP and validated traction, we’ll introduce you to relevant grant programs, angel investors, and pre-seed funds. Our mentors help you refine your pitch, and we host a demo day at the end of the program for you to present to potential backers.
